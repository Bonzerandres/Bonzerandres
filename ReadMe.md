# 💫 About Me:
# Hi, I'm Andres Del Orbe<br><br>I'm a data engineer focused on building practical data platforms, automated pipelines, and cloud systems that solve real business problems.<br><br>My work sits between data engineering, analytics engineering, cloud infrastructure, and FinOps. I care a lot about choosing the right tool for the job. Sometimes that means Snowflake or Databricks. Sometimes it means DuckDB, MotherDuck, Postgres, S3, or a simpler serverless architecture because the workload does not need unnecessary complexity.<br><br>I like building systems that are clean, maintainable, cost aware, and easy to explain.<br><br>## About Me<br><br>🔭 I'm currently working on  <br>Building data engineering projects around economic data, cloud cost intelligence, and operational automation. My main focus is turning messy data sources into reliable pipelines, clean models, and useful dashboards.<br><br>👯 I'm looking to collaborate on  <br>Data engineering, analytics engineering, dbt projects, DuckDB/MotherDuck workflows, Snowflake pipelines, cloud data platforms, and AI-assisted operations tools.<br><br>🤝 I'm looking for help with  <br>Improving production-grade patterns around orchestration, data observability, data contracts, and open source contribution workflows.<br><br>🌱 I'm currently learning  <br>Advanced dbt, Snowflake, Databricks, data orchestration, FinOps automation, data quality testing, and how to design better end-to-end data platforms.<br><br>💬 Ask me about  <br>SQL, Python, AWS, dbt, DuckDB, MotherDuck, Snowflake, data lakes, ETL/ELT pipelines, FinOps, CI/CD, and how to turn fragmented business systems into usable data platforms.<br><br>⚡ Fun fact  <br>I think about data infrastructure the same way I think about finance: every system has cost, risk, latency, and tradeoffs. The best solution is not always the biggest one. It is the one that fits the problem.<br><br>## Selected Projects<br><br>### Recession Signal Monitoring Platform<br><br>I built a data pipeline that tracks how close the U.S. economy may be to recession by combining multiple economic indicators instead of relying on one delayed signal.<br><br>The idea was simple: standard economic reports are often lagging, so I wanted a near real-time way to track recession risk through automated data ingestion, transformation, scoring, and backtesting.<br><br>**What it does**<br>- Ingests economic indicators from FRED<br>- Tracks multiple recession-related signals<br>- Scores how many indicators are flashing red<br>- Backtests signals against historical recessions<br>- Produces analytics-ready datasets for dashboards and reporting<br><br>**Stack**<br>- Python<br>- SQL<br>- DuckDB<br>- MotherDuck<br>- dbt<br>- Hex<br>- FRED API<br><br>**What I focused on**<br>- Cost-effective architecture<br>- dbt staging and mart models<br>- Data quality checks<br>- Lineage and documentation<br>- Reproducible analytics workflows<br>- Practical tradeoffs between lightweight analytics tools and larger cloud warehouses<br><br>This project reflects how I think as a data engineer: do not overbuild just to use a popular tool. Pick the architecture that fits the workload, keep it maintainable, and leave a clear path to scale when the data actually demands it.<br><br>---<br><br>### Agentic FinOps Intelligence Platform<br><br>This project combines data engineering, cloud cost visibility, and AI-assisted operations.<br><br>The goal is to build a platform that helps teams understand where cloud costs are coming from, detect waste, and turn infrastructure spending into something measurable and explainable.<br><br>**What it does**<br>- Ingests cloud billing and usage data<br>- Organizes cost data into bronze, silver, and gold layers<br>- Builds cost analytics models<br>- Supports forecasting and optimization workflows<br>- Creates a foundation for AI agents to explain cloud spend and recommend actions<br><br>**Stack**<br>- AWS<br>- S3<br>- Athena<br>- DuckDB<br>- dbt<br>- Python<br>- SQL<br>- Terraform<br>- GitHub Actions<br>- Grafana or Retool<br><br>**What I focused on**<br>- FinOps thinking<br>- Data modeling for cost intelligence<br>- Cloud cost governance<br>- Automated reporting<br>- Infrastructure as code<br>- Data-driven decision making<br><br>This project is important to me because it shows that data engineering is not only about moving data. It is about helping teams make better operational and financial decisions.<br><br>---<br><br>### ATR Platform Unification<br><br>I worked on a platform modernization plan for a company with fragmented operations across CRM, accounting, warehouse, support, e-commerce, logistics, and reporting systems.<br><br>The problem was not just technical. The real issue was that business operations were spread across disconnected tools, spreadsheets, manual exports, and processes that did not communicate with each other.<br><br>**What it does**<br>- Maps fragmented business systems into a unified platform vision<br>- Designs the future data and workflow architecture<br>- Connects operational modules like CRM, warehouse, support, accounts, logistics, and reporting<br>- Reduces dependency on disconnected spreadsheets and manual processes<br>- Supports Spanish-language business users and operational teams<br><br>**Stack and concepts**<br>- Data platform architecture<br>- ERP modernization<br>- CRM workflows<br>- Warehouse and logistics data<br>- Operational reporting<br>- System integration planning<br>- Documentation and process mapping<br><br>**What I focused on**<br>- Turning messy operations into structured systems<br>- Understanding stakeholder workflows<br>- Designing data models around real business processes<br>- Creating a realistic migration plan instead of forcing a big-bang rebuild<br>- Keeping the business running while modernizing the stack<br><br>This project shaped the way I think about data engineering. A good data platform is not just a database or a pipeline. It is the operational backbone that lets people trust the data they use every day.<br><br>---<br><br>### Scarlet Visitor Management and Building Security Platform<br><br>Scarlet is a visitor-management and building-security platform built for a real-world housing authority use case.<br><br>The project involved sensitive identity, access, visitor, and ban-management data. That meant the data layer had to be designed with validation, permissions, security, and reliability in mind from the start.<br><br>**What it does**<br>- Supports visitor vetting<br>- Handles mobile ID scanning workflows<br>- Manages visitor and ban records<br>- Stores and validates identity/access data<br>- Provides the foundation for building-security operations<br><br>**Stack and concepts**<br>- Application architecture<br>- Data persistence layer<br>- Access controls<br>- CI/CD workflows<br>- Jira Kanban delivery<br>- Security-focused data modeling<br><br>**What I focused on**<br>- Data integrity<br>- Access control<br>- Sensitive data handling<br>- Maintainable delivery workflows<br>- Owning the full lifecycle from discovery to production<br><br>This project gave me experience building systems where data quality is not optional. When the data is tied to safety and access decisions, the pipeline and persistence layer have to be reliable.<br><br>---<br><br>### Functional Safety Evidence Pipeline<br><br>I have also been exploring how data engineering and DevOps can support regulated engineering environments, especially around ISO 26262-style evidence automation.<br><br>The idea is to automate the evidence chain that safety and engineering teams usually assemble manually.<br><br>**What it is designed to do**<br>- Connect requirements, tests, coverage, and validation artifacts<br>- Generate traceability outputs<br>- Support audit-ready reporting<br>- Bring CI/CD discipline into safety evidence workflows<br>- Help engineers reduce manual certification paperwork<br><br>**Stack and concepts**<br>- Python<br>- SQL<br>- CI/CD<br>- Traceability matrices<br>- Automated validation<br>- Documentation generation<br>- Regulated-environment workflows<br><br>This project reflects a bigger theme in my work: I like building the automation layer underneath complex teams. I am not trying to replace domain experts. I want to give them cleaner systems, better evidence, and less manual work.<br><br>## Core Skills<br><br>**Languages and data**<br>Python, SQL, Pandas, NumPy, PostgreSQL, Snowflake, BigQuery, DuckDB, MotherDuck<br><br>**Data engineering**<br>ETL, ELT, data modeling, data warehousing, data lakes, dbt, data quality, validation, lineage, documentation<br><br>**Cloud and infrastructure**<br>AWS, S3, Glue, Athena, Lambda, Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, Linux<br><br>**Pipelines and automation**<br>GitHub Actions, Jenkins, CI/CD, Shell scripting, automated tests, workflow automation<br><br>**Governance and reliability**<br>Access controls, DevSecOps scanning, data security, observability, cost visibility, FinOps<br><br>## How I Think About Engineering<br><br>I do not believe every problem needs the most complex architecture available.<br><br>I believe in understanding the workload first, then choosing the right tool.<br><br>For smaller analytical workloads, I might use DuckDB or MotherDuck because they are fast, simple, and cost effective. For larger enterprise workloads, I understand why tools like Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery, and distributed systems become necessary.<br><br>That mindset is how I keep learning. I do not learn tools just because they are popular. I learn them because I understand what problem they solve, where they fit, and when they become the right choice.<br><br>## What I Am Building Toward<br><br>I am building toward roles in:<br><br>- Data Engineering<br>- Analytics Engineering<br>- Data Platform Engineering<br>- Cloud Data Infrastructure<br>- FinOps Data Engineering<br>- AI-assisted operations and automation<br><br>My goal is to keep building systems that are useful, explainable, and production-minded.
